DSLR - Digital Single-Lens Reflex

What are they?

When it comes to finding out what a DSLR camrea is this website is the correct place to find out, a DLSR camrea means Digital Single-lens Reflex Camrea - this means that the camrea's insides houses a mirror that refects light coming from the camreas lens and sends it through the prizm and then into the optical viewfinder, some lower-end DSLR's run the light through series of mirrors where as the higher-end camreas just have the one. This also shows the image that your taking a photo of.

When the shutter is pressed, the mirror inside the camera flips up which is where the term reflex comes into play — and sends the light coming through the lens directly to the sensor instead of the viewfinder. The advantage of this setup, compared to that of a mirrorless or point-and-shoot camera, is that you can see, in real-time, the exact scene you’re going to capture. There’s no lag, as there tends to be with point-and-shoots and mirrorless cameras where the sensor has to transfer what it’s seeing to a separate digital display
